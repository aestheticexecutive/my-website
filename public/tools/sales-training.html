Stage 1
The First Impression

Speed to Lead

The moment someone reaches out is the highest point of their interest. Every minute you wait, that interest — and your chance — diminishes. Your goal: respond within 5 minutes, every time.

<5
Minute response window for maximum conversion probability
7
Total touchpoints before moving to cold outreach
The 7-Touch Outreach Sequence
1
Call immediately
2
Text if no answer
3
Call next day
4
Text next day
5
Call 3–7 days later
6
Text 3–7 days later
7
Final text + incentive
  • Use the patient's first name — every time
  • Add a personal touch: "I saw you reached out about Morpheus8 — I'd love to answer any questions!"
  • Include a before-and-after photo relevant to their inquiry to spark engagement
  • Share your practice mission or core values: "At [Practice], we believe..."
  • Keep it conversational — no corporate language, no copy-paste scripts that feel robotic
Example Text — First Outreach
"Hi [Name]! This is [Your Name] from [Practice]. I saw you were interested in learning more about your skin — I'd love to chat! Here's a quick before-and-after from one of our recent treatments 👆 When's a good time to connect?"
Final Touch (Day 10–14) — Incentive Text
"Hi [Name], I wanted to reach out one more time — we'd love to have you in. As a thank-you for booking this month, I'm happy to offer $50 toward your first service. Would love to get you on the schedule! 💫"
  • Answer with energy and warmth — your tone sets the entire relationship
  • Have a distinctive, memorable greeting: "Thank you for calling [Practice] — where every detail matters!"
  • Never let it ring more than twice during business hours without a plan in place for callbacks
  • If you miss a call: call back within 5 minutes, then text immediately after
  • The goal of the call is not just to answer questions — it's to book a consultation
Stage 2
On the Phone

Booking the Consultation

This is where you shift from inquiry to commitment. Brand your consultation, ask the right questions, and close the booking — confidently and naturally.

Don't book a "Morpheus8 consultation." Book your Signature Skin Assessment — a personalized experience that includes a skin analysis, imaging, body composition review, and a customized treatment plan.

Brand Your Consultation — Name It, Own It

Create a name for your consultation that feels premium and exclusive — patients should want to come in because the consult itself sounds valuable.

  • Name it: "Your Signature Skin Assessment," "The Radiance Consultation," "Your Complete Beauty Blueprint"
  • Tell them what's included: Skin analysis, body composition, 3D imaging, provider review, customized treatment plan
  • Set expectations: How long it is, what to bring, what they'll walk away with
  • Build anticipation: "You're going to love this — we take a really thorough approach here"
  • What areas are you most interested in treating?
  • What's your main concern — and how long have you been dealing with it?
  • What have you tried before? Did it work?
  • What does your ideal result look like?
  • Is there a specific event or timeline you're working toward?
  • How did you hear about us?
  • Is there anything specific you want to make sure we cover during your visit?

Add all notes to the appointment — the provider should walk in already knowing their story.

Assuming the Booking
"Our next available is [Day] at [Time] — would that work for you?"

Don't give them your entire calendar. Lead with one strong option — it creates a sense of demand without saying you're fully booked.

If That Time Doesn't Work
"No problem — what days and times generally work best for you? Let me see what we have that lines up."
Always Close With
"Is there anything else I can help you with before we see you on [Date]?"
Stage 3
Before They Arrive

The Pre-Consult Experience

The consultation experience starts before they walk in. A thoughtful confirmation call and a personalized provider bio can set the entire tone.

📋
The Confirmation Call
Confirm day, time, and location. Share any parking or access details. Ask if there's a specific part of the consultation they want to spend extra time on — skincare, tech, payment plans. Document everything.
👤
The Provider Bio Text
Send a personalized text with your provider's credentials, experience, and a personal note. Include a photo if possible. This breaks the ice before they arrive and builds immediate trust.
Sample Provider Bio Text
"Hi [Name]! 👋 We're so excited to see you on [Day]. You'll be meeting with [Provider Name] — she has [X] years of experience in aesthetic medicine and has performed over [#] treatments. Outside of work, she loves [personal detail]. We know you're in great hands. See you soon!"
  • Design forms to surface concerns, goals, and history — not just basic info
  • If digital completion rates are low, switch to paper or have staff complete it with the patient
  • Providers should review intake form responses before every appointment — every time
  • Use intake data to personalize the consultation opening
  • Revisit and update forms at each visit to track how concerns evolve
Stage 4
In the Room

The Consultation

The best consultations feel like conversations, not sales pitches. Let them talk first. Listen deeply. Then step into your role as the expert they need.

The provider's job in the first half of the consultation is to ask questions — not answer them. The more the patient talks, the more trust you build, and the more precise your recommendations become.

Core Consultation Philosophy
Opening Question 1 — The Door Opener
"So, what brought you in today?"
Opening Question 2 — Treatment History
"What have you tried before to address this?"
Opening Question 3 — Expectations
"What type of results would make you feel really happy with this?"

After each answer — keep going. Don't rush to solutions. Ask follow-up questions and let them fill the silence.

  • Do you have a specific event or timeline you're working toward?
  • What made you finally decide to pick up the phone and book this?
  • How long have you been thinking about addressing this?
  • What skincare products are you currently using?
  • What does your day-to-day routine look like?
  • What do you do for work?
  • Have you visited other practices for aesthetic treatments? What did you love — or not love?
  • What, if anything, are you currently doing to address or cover this concern?

After listening, transition into evaluation mode. The best providers aren't yes-people — they evaluate, they recommend, and they protect outcomes. This builds credibility and makes patients want to be a candidate.

Candidacy Bridge Script
"We have a lot of amazing transformative treatments here — but not every treatment is right for every person. So I'd love to do a really thorough evaluation and see exactly what I think would get you to your goals."

This positions you as a trusted expert — not a salesperson. It also makes patients lean in and want your approval.

Give them the mirror. Let them point to their concerns. Then respond with a compliment sandwich — affirm something genuine, address the concern, and close with a positive outcome vision.

Compliment Sandwich Example
"Wow, you have a really incredible cheekbone structure — I love that. I do see the fine lines you're pointing to around your eyes. I honestly think if we address those, it's going to make your eyes look so much brighter and really bring out all that beautiful bone structure you already have."
  • Pull out before-and-afters that are relevant to their specific concern and treatment
  • Walk through results conversationally — "Here's what we got for this patient who had a similar concern..."
  • Credential yourself naturally: "I've done hundreds of these procedures — the outcomes are really consistent when we follow the right protocol."
  • Highlight what makes your technology or technique different from what they might have seen elsewhere
  • Invite them to ask questions as you go — keep it a dialogue, not a presentation
Stage 5
The Moment of Truth

Price, Plans & Objections

You've built trust and shown results. Now step out, build the plan, come back, and present it like the expert you are — with confidence, not apology.

  • Treatment name — spell it out, make it feel real
  • Number of recommended sessions
  • What it treats — specific to their concern
  • Downtime expectations
  • Prep and aftercare notes
  • Price — already on the paper, so you don't have to "say" it
  • At-home care section — products or habits that will enhance outcomes

If you've recommended products, bring them into the room in a bag. Let them see, touch, and hold what's in the plan.

Opening the Presentation
"Okay, so here's what I've put together for you — I'm really excited about this plan. Let me walk you through it."
Presenting Each Treatment
"So first, I'd start with [Treatment]. This addresses [concern] by [brief how it works]. Before we do it, you'll want to [prep]. Afterward you can expect [recovery]. And I'd recommend [# of sessions] for the results we're going for."
Closing the Presentation
"What questions do you have?" ... "Any other questions?" ... "Perfect — let's head up front and get your first treatment on the schedule."

Send them home with a copy of the plan. Take a photo or scan it yourself so you can text it later.

Objection Handling
Common Objections — Tap to See the Response

Totally understand — and I want to make sure you understand what's included in that. You're not just paying for a treatment — you're paying for my expertise, the protocol, and the results. Not all treatments are the same. The technique used, the depth, the settings — those things make a massive difference in outcome. Our pricing reflects the level of care and precision you'll get here.

Of course! What questions do you think they might have? Let me answer those now so you have everything you need to make the decision together. I want you to feel completely confident going into this.

I hear you — and price is always a fair consideration. What I'd encourage you to ask when comparing is: What technology are they using? What protocol do they follow? How many of these has the provider done? Because the machine, the depth, the technique — those all determine your results. We invest heavily in the highest-quality technology and training, and that's reflected in the consistency of our outcomes.

No pressure at all! When would you ideally want to start? I'd love to at least get your first appointment on the calendar now — even if it's a few months out — so we can make sure you get the time slot that works best for you. We tend to book out quickly, especially on weekends.

Absolutely not! This is your full plan — your roadmap. We can absolutely prioritize and build toward it over time. What feels most important to you to address first? Let's start there and go from there.

Stage 6
After They Leave

The Follow-Up Sequence

Most practices lose patients between the consult and the first treatment. Your follow-up sequence is the bridge. Four touches, done right, can dramatically change your conversion rate.

~2h
Same Day — The Treatment Plan Text
Text them a photo of their treatment plan. Keep it warm and personal.
Example
"Hi [Name]! It was so great meeting you today. I'm really excited about the plan we put together — here's a photo of it so you always have it! Please reach out with any questions anytime. 💛"
D+2
2 Days After — The Check-In
Check in, see if they have questions, gently re-offer booking.
Example
"Hi [Name]! Just wanted to check in and see if you had any questions about your treatment plan. We'd love to get your first appointment on the books whenever you're ready!"
D+7
1 Week After — The Soft Ask
Acknowledge they've had time to think. Offer a quick call to address anything that came up.
Example
"Hi [Name]! Now that you've had a week to think things over, I'd love to set up a quick 10–15 minute call to answer any questions that came up. How does [Day] at [Time] sound?"
D+14
2 Weeks After — The Final Touch
One last personal reach out. Keep it light. Offer to align on a future timeline if they're not ready.
Example
"Hey [Name]! Haven't heard back — just wanted to make sure everything was okay! If you're thinking about doing this a little later in the year, totally understand — I'd love to know your ideal timeline so we can make sure to reach out and get you on the schedule."
📧
After the Follow-Up Sequence
If they haven't converted after the four-touch sequence, move them into your regular marketing pipeline — email campaigns, promotions, newsletters. They've already been through a consult with you. They're warm. Keep showing up.
